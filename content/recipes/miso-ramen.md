## Timing

- **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
- **Cook Time:** 15 minutes
- **Total Time:** 30 minutes
- **Servings:** 2 Bowls

## Ingredients

- 2 garlic cloves (finely minced or grated)
- 1 inch ginger knob (finely minced or grated)
- 1 shallot (finely minced or grated)
- 1 Tbsp white sesame seeds (toasted & ground)
- 1 Tbsp sesame oil
- ¼ lb ground pork
- 1 tsp Doubanjiang (spicy chili bean paste)
- 3 Tbsp miso (awase)
- 1 Tbsp sugar
- 1 Tbsp sake
- 4 cups chicken stock
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- ¼ tsp white pepper (finely ground)
- 2 servings fresh ramen noodles
- 2 ramen eggs ([see recipe here]({{< ref "recipes/ramen-egg" >}}))
- 3-6 slices of chashu pork belly ([see recipe here]({{< ref "recipes/sous-vide-chashu-pork" >}}))

## Instructions

1. In a pot, heat sesame oil over medium heat and cook the garlic, ginger, & shallot until fragrant
2. Add the meat and increase heat to medium-high, mashing and stirring until no longer pink
3. Add the spicy bean paste and miso and mix well
4. Add the sesame seeds and sugar and mix well
5. Add the sake, chicken stock, salt, & pepper and bring to a simmer
6. Cook the ramen noodles in salty water as directed on the package
7. Drain the noodles very well and place in a warmed bowl
8. Top the noodles in the bowl with the soup along with any other toppings
