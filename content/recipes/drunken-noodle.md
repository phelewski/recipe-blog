## Timing

- **Prep Time:** 15 minutes
- **Cook Time:** 15 minutes
- **Total Time:** 30 minutes
- **Servings:** 2 adult portions

## Ingredients

- 1 Tbsp fish sauce
- 1 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1 Tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 Tbsp oyster sauce
- 3 Tbsp vegetable oil, plus extra
- 8oz. boneless, skinless, chicken thighs (cut into Â½" pieces)
- 1 large shallot (sliced thinly)
- kosher salt to taste
- 8 garlic cloves (sliced thinly)
- 1-2 Thai chilies (sliced thinly) (optional)
- 8oz. wide/broad dried rice noodles (rehydrated to package directions)
- 1 cup Thai basil leaves

## Instructions

1. In a small bowl, whisk the fish sauce, soy sauces, and oyster sauce until combined. Set aside until ready to use.
2. In a seasoned wok set over high heat, add the oil and swirl to coat the bottom and sides. Once the oil begins to smoke, add the chicken pieces and sear for 2 minutes without disturbing.
3. Stirring often, cook for an additional 4-5 minutes, then transfer chicken to a bowl
4. Add another 2 Tbsp oil to the wok and heat until smoking. Add the shallots and season slightly with salt. Stir fry until the shallots begin to turn translucent, about 2 minutes.
5. Add the garlic and saute another minute (Add chilies now if using them)
6. Add the rehydrated noodles along with the chicken to the wok and stir fry for another 1-2 minutes, tossing everything to combine
7. Add the sauce and toss to evenly coat the noodles and cook for another minute
8. Turn off the heat and add the Thai basil, gently tossing to evenly distribute
9. Transfer to serving bowl
